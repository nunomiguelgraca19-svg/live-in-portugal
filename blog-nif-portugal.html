How to Get a NIF in Portugal: 2026 Guide for Foreigners

Updated March 2026 · 5 min read

Quick summary

• A NIF is your 9-digit Portuguese tax number. You need it before you can rent, buy, open a bank account, or sign any contract.

• You can get it in person at any tax office (5–10 minutes) or remotely through a fiscal representative (3–10 business days).

• The NIF itself is free. Fiscal representative services typically cost €50–€150.

What is a NIF and why do you need one?

NIF stands for Número de Identificação Fiscal — your Portuguese tax identification number. It’s a simple 9-digit code, and it’s the very first thing you need when doing anything official in Portugal.

You’ll need your NIF to rent or buy a property, open a bank account, sign a work contract, set up utilities (electricity, water, internet), register with the health service, or file taxes. Essentially, nothing moves forward without it.

Who needs a fiscal representative?

If you’re a non-EU/EEA citizen and don’t yet have a Portuguese address, you’re required to appoint a fiscal representative — someone with a Portuguese address who acts as your tax contact. This person doesn’t need to be a lawyer or accountant; any Portuguese resident can do it, though most people use a professional service for convenience.

EU/EEA citizens can often get a NIF without a fiscal representative, especially if they have an address in Portugal (even a hotel address has worked in some cases). However, rules are applied inconsistently across different tax offices.

Good to know: Since 2023, the requirement for a fiscal representative has been relaxed for EU/EEA residents. But if you’re from outside the EU, you still need one. This is unlikely to change soon.

Option A: Get your NIF in person

If you’re already in Portugal (or planning a visit), this is the fastest route.

1 Gather your documents

You need a valid passport (or EU ID card) and proof of address in your home country (utility bill or bank statement, ideally translated). If you need a fiscal representative, they should come with you or provide a signed authorisation letter.

2 Visit a Finanças office or Loja do Cidadão

Go to any tax office (Serviço de Finanças) or Citizen Shop. In Lisbon, the busiest offices are Finanças de Lisboa 3 (Av. Eng. Duarte Pacheco) and the Loja do Cidadão in Laranjeiras. Smaller offices outside the capital tend to be quicker. You don’t need an appointment, but arrive early — queues build up from mid-morning.

3 Receive your NIF

The NIF is typically issued on the spot, within 5–10 minutes. You’ll get a printed document with your number. Keep it safe — you’ll use it constantly.

Option B: Get your NIF remotely

If you’re not yet in Portugal, you can apply through a fiscal representative who handles the process on your behalf.

1 Choose a fiscal representative

This can be a lawyer, accountant, or specialised service. Many companies offer this online for €50–€150. They’ll act as your tax address in Portugal until you have your own.

2 Send your documents

Typically: a scan of your passport, proof of address in your home country, and a signed power of attorney for the fiscal representative (a simple form, not a notarised document).

3 Wait for your NIF

The representative submits the application at the tax office. Processing takes 3–10 business days. You receive your NIF by email.

How much does it cost?

The NIF itself is free — the Portuguese state doesn’t charge anything for issuing one. If you use a fiscal representative service, expect to pay €50–€150 depending on the provider. Some services also charge an annual fee (€50–€100/year) for maintaining the fiscal representation.

Tip: Once you have a Portuguese address (rental contract), you can cancel the fiscal representative and update your address at Finanças. This stops the annual fee.

Common pitfalls

Inconsistent rules. Different tax offices may ask for slightly different documents. If you’re refused at one office, try another — it happens more often than you’d think.

Address requirements. Some offices accept a hotel address, others don’t. If you’re an EU citizen without a Portuguese address, bring proof of accommodation (hotel booking, Airbnb confirmation) just in case.

Fiscal representative scams. Use established companies or professionals recommended by other expats. Avoid anyone who asks for unusually high fees or requests access to your bank account.
